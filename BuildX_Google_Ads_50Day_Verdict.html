At $50/Day, You Don't Run 5 Campaigns.
You Run One — Well.

You asked the right question. Splitting $50/day across five campaigns is the #1 way small budgets get wasted. Here's the math on why, and the phased structure that actually serves all five of your goals without starving the only one that pays the bills.

✕

The 5-campaign plan was built for a bigger budget.

Google's bidding AI needs ~30–50 conversions per month per campaign to learn. At $50/day you can barely feed one campaign that much. Five campaigns = five starving learners, none of them ever gets smart, and your money evaporates on clicks that don't close.

✕ Split 5 ways
Non-Brand Search~$25/day
Video views~$10/day
Demand Gen~$10/day
Brand~$5/day
Clicks to the money campaign~4/day
→ Never hits learning threshold. No PCC data. Flatline.
✓ Concentrated
Non-Brand Search (ADU → PCC)~$47/day
Brand (capped)~$3/day
Est. ADU-vertical CPC*~$4–7
Clicks to the money campaign~7–11/day
→ ~15–27 PCC/mo possible. Enough to actually optimize.

* Estimate — pull your real CPC from Keyword Planner before launch. All-industry avg is ~$5.42 in 2026; home-services/contractor runs $3–7.

TL;DR Run 1 campaign now. Add the rest as rewards.
Campaign count at a small budget isn't decided by how many goals you have — it's capped by how many conversions you can generate. You can feed one. So the five goals get served in sequence and by overlap, not in parallel.
NOW · $50/day

1 Search campaign: ADU Non-Brand → Let's Talk

This single campaign does double duty: it's your "ADU search domination" goal AND your "PCC bookings" goal — because they're literally the same thing. Someone searches "adu builder massachusetts," clicks, lands on Let's Talk, submits. That's both goals in one motion.

NOW · ~$3/day carve-out

+ Tiny Brand campaign (capped)

Optional but cheap. Protects "BuildX" from competitors once your podcast + Meta ads create branded searches. Barely spends. Only add once you confirm people actually search your name.

UNLOCK · ~15–30 PCC/mo

+ Demand Gen retargeting → BuildX.com traffic

Your "website traffic" goal. Waits because it's cheap reach that's wasted until you have warm audiences to retarget. By the time you unlock it, your Search campaign + organic will have filled the retargeting pool.

UNLOCK · budget ≥ $100/day

+ Video views campaign → YouTube growth

Your "YouTube views" goal. Waits because at $50/day, paying for views steals from the only campaign closing leads — and your content engine (Meta + organic + podcast) already drives views for free.

⚠ SIGNAL CHECK — The insight that reframes everything
Only 2 of your 5 goals actually need paid dollars right now, and they're the same campaign. YouTube views and Learning Center SEO aren't paid-ad problems at this budget — they're content and SEO problems your existing engine already handles. Website traffic is mostly a free byproduct of the Search campaign. Don't pay for what you're already getting.

02 Build the One Campaign That Matters
Tracking first — the bidding AI is blind without it (your fbclid/CAPI lesson, Google edition). Then one lean, high-intent Search campaign.
✓

Verify the PCC conversion fires BEFORE spending a dollar

Install the Google tag, create the primary conversion on the HubSpot form-submit / thank-you state (not the page view), then submit a test and confirm it registers. A broken conversion at this budget means 100% of your money optimizes to noise.

🖱 Goals → Conversions → +New → "PCC Form Submit"✅ Test submit + verify
✓

Lock geo to presence-only, served towns

Radius over Plymouth County / South Shore / Upper Cape. Change Google's default from "Presence or interest" to "Presence: people in or regularly in your locations" — the default will spend your tiny budget on someone in Florida "interested in" Massachusetts.

✓

Keep it to 2–3 tight ad groups, high-intent only

At this budget, a focused list of 30–50 intent-rich keywords beats a sprawl of 500 every time. Skip the town-by-town and broad research terms for now — just the hottest buyer intent:

AG1 · Builder intent → Let's Talk

"adu builder" "adu builder massachusetts" "adu contractor" "accessory dwelling unit builder" "adu builder south shore"

AG2 · In-law / family intent → Let's Talk

"in law suite builder" "in law apartment builder" "granny flat builder massachusetts" "multigenerational home addition"

AG3 · Cost intent → Learning Center article (not Let's Talk)

"adu cost massachusetts" "how much does an adu cost" "cost to build in law suite"
✓

Match type: phrase + exact. NOT broad (yet).

Google will push broad match + Smart Bidding hard. That combo needs 30–50 conversions of history to be safe — you don't have it. Launch phrase/exact to protect every dollar, graduate to broad later once data exists.

✓

Load negatives before launch — non-negotiable on a small budget

One wasted click is a bigger % of $50 than of $500. Block researchers, renters, and job seekers at the campaign level day one:

diy plans kit kits prefab free rent rental jobs salary career laws zoning permit application what is an adu course training
✓

Bidding: start Maximize Clicks (capped), then graduate

Max Clicks with a max-CPC cap gathers data at the lowest cost while you learn. After ~30–50 conversions, move to Maximize Conversions, then Target CPA once you know your real cost per PCC. Don't start at Target CPA — the AI has no data to hit a target with.

✓

RSAs in BuildX voice, ADU keyword pinned to Headline 1

15 headlines / 4 descriptions per group, pulled from proven Meta hook lanes: price myth-bust ($274K), contractor-vetting, 100-day guarantee, walk-one-first / open tour. Same honest, no-slogan rules as your Facebook copy. No "all-electric," no RSVP language.

⚠ SIGNAL CHECK — Don't touch it for 2 weeks (except negatives)
New campaigns need 30–60 days to learn — judging it after a few days and flipping it off tanks your momentum and Quality Score. The one daily job in week one: read the Search Terms report and add negatives. Everything else, leave alone and let it learn.
03 The Unlock Ladder
You earn more campaigns — you don't launch them on hope. Each rung unlocks only when the data or the budget supports it. This is how you scale without ever starving the core.
✓

Rung 1 · Add Brand campaign — when people search "BuildX"

Trigger: you see branded searches showing up (podcast + Meta will create them). Cost: cap it at ~$3–5/day; it'll rarely spend that. Cheapest, highest-converting clicks in the account.

✓

Rung 2 · Add Demand Gen retargeting — at ~15–30 PCC/mo

Trigger: Search is producing steady PCCs AND your audiences have filled. This is your "BuildX.com traffic" goal done right — warm retargeting to site visitors + YouTube viewers, running your Meta creative. Fund it with a small budget bump or by trimming Search's lowest ad group, not by splitting the core.

✓

Rung 3 · Add Video views — at budget ≥ $100/day

Trigger: total budget can afford it without touching Search. This is your "YouTube views" goal. CPVs are pennies, it builds the awareness pool, and your Descript hook-mining is exactly the first-5-seconds skill it needs. Judge it on view rate + audience growth, never on cost per PCC.

✓

Rung 4 · Test Performance Max — at ~20–30 PCC/mo, budget ≥ $150/day

Trigger: enough conversion volume to feed it (rule of thumb: daily budget ≥ 15× target CPA) and proven ROI to protect. Feed it your best Meta assets in all 5 aspect ratios + Customer Match signals, and set brand exclusions so it doesn't cannibalize the Brand campaign.

⚠ SIGNAL CHECK — The one number that means "domination"
Watch Impression Share on your non-brand ADU keywords. Search-term dominance literally IS this number climbing toward the top of the auction. When it's high and cost-per-PCC is under your ~$18.75 Meta benchmark, that's your signal to climb the next rung. Build the monthly Google Ads Excel SOP mirroring your Meta one — Cost per PCC as the hero metric.
